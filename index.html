The Doris Duke Foundation and the Performing Arts Technologies Lab

The Doris Duke Foundation announced grants to 20 innovative theater, jazz, contemporary dance, and multi-disciplinary projects through its inaugural Performing Arts Technologies Lab on September 25, 2024. Artists, technologists, and arts organizations receive funding and support to develop new uses for digital technologies across the performing arts.

For full details, visit the Doris Duke Foundation's announcement.

Grants and Support for Innovation

The Performing Arts Technologies Lab supports projects that integrate technology into the performing arts, offering grants to foster experimentation with digital tools. The grant provides opportunities for artists to work with technologists to explore new ways of storytelling, performance, and audience engagement.

Schools and Organizations Involved

The Juilliard School

Exploring how AI and motion-tracking technologies can assist dancers in choreography creation, technique enhancement, and performance analytics.

California Institute of the Arts (CalArts)

Investigating how VR and AR can create immersive dance environments where performers interact with digital counterparts.

New York University (NYU)

Combining theater, dance, and music with technologies like AI, robotics, and multimedia storytelling to enhance audience engagement and artistic expression.

Technological Impact on Performing Arts

AI is reshaping dance by assisting in choreography, analyzing motion patterns, and providing real-time feedback. Cross-disciplinary collaborations are creating rich, multi-disciplinary works that integrate dancers, musicians, and technologists.

Featured Project: Totentanz (T3)

About Totentanz (T3)

Totentanz, also known as T3, is a new use of pose estimation and facial recognition technologies to decrease AI theft and training that occurs without choreographer and dancer consent. This innovative solution aims to increase ownership and creative agency of artists.

Our Team and Sponsorship

This solution was devised by Pedram Nimreezi, an AI engineer, and Nicole Perry, a dance and theatre artist. Momentum Stage is sponsoring the project, and it is funded through the inaugural “Performing Arts and Technologies Lab” grant from the Doris Duke Charitable Foundation.
